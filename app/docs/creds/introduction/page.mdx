# Introduction to Decentralized Identity  


## Overview  

The digital world relies heavily on identity systems for authentication, authorization, and trust. However, most traditional identity systems are **centralized**, controlled by corporations, governments, or third-party providers. This model creates **privacy risks, security vulnerabilities, and data monopolies** that restrict users‚Äô control over their identities.

Decentralized Identity (**DID**) is a paradigm shift that **eliminates central points of failure**, allowing individuals and organizations to **own, control, and share** their identities securely, without relying on a single authority.

Ezrah is building a **blockchain-powered identity infrastructure** that provides a **self-sovereign, verifiable, and interoperable identity system**, ensuring greater security, privacy, and autonomy in the digital world.

---

## The Problem with Traditional Identity Systems  

Most identity models today rely on **centralized databases** controlled by governments, banks, social networks, or enterprises. This structure leads to several **critical issues**:

### Lack of User Control
- Users depend on third-party platforms to create and manage identities (e.g., Google, Facebook, government agencies).  
- Accounts can be **revoked, suspended, or hacked** without the user‚Äôs consent.  
- No **cross-platform portability**‚Äîa Facebook ID cannot be used on LinkedIn or a banking app.

### Privacy Risks & Data Exploitation
- Companies **collect and monetize user data**, often without transparent consent.  
- Centralized identity providers store vast amounts of **personal information**, making them prime targets for cyberattacks.  
- **Mass surveillance & tracking** erode individual privacy rights.

### Security Vulnerabilities & Identity Theft
- **Data breaches** expose millions of identities every year.  
- **Phishing attacks** exploit weak authentication mechanisms.  
- Fraudsters can easily **fake or steal identities** due to weak verification processes.

### Compliance & Regulatory Challenges
- KYC (Know Your Customer) & AML (Anti-Money Laundering) regulations require businesses to verify identities, leading to **costly and fragmented compliance processes**.  
- Companies must **store user data**, increasing **legal risks** under GDPR, CCPA, and other regulations.

## **What is Decentralized Identity?**  

Decentralized Identity (**DID**) is a **trustless** and **self-sovereign** identity model that removes the need for centralized authorities.  

### **Core Principles of Decentralized Identity**  
**Self-Sovereignty** ‚Äì Individuals **own and control** their identities.  
**Interoperability** ‚Äì IDs can be used across different platforms and ecosystems.  
**Privacy-Preserving** ‚Äì Minimal personal data exposure using **zero-knowledge proofs (ZKPs)**.  
**Security & Verifiability** ‚Äì Identities are **tamper-proof and cryptographically secured**.

1Ô∏è‚É£ **Decentralized Identifiers (DIDs)**  
A **DID** is a **unique, cryptographic identifier** stored on a blockchain. Unlike traditional usernames (email, phone number), DIDs are:  
- **Immutable**: Cannot be changed or revoked by any central authority.  
- **Verifiable**: Ownership can be proven using cryptographic signatures.  
- **Interoperable**: Can be used across multiple platforms.  

Example of a **DID URL**:
